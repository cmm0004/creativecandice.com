Aruvio UI Design

For my work with aruvio.com, I was tasked with increasing our organic search visits by enhancing our SEO and providing a better User Interface for the company website. These changes have been incremental and iterative, but since I've come on-board, some very clear and important changes have occurred.

View Live Site

The Home Page Slider

Among small changes, like making the logo bigger, adding contact information across the top, adding side navigation, and re-arranging the contents of the header menu, the biggest change to the homepage has been the slider

When I came onboard, the slider was stock potos of business people and a call to submit your information for a demo, but the user wouldn't even have had time to know what Aruvio was selling, why would they submit thier information?

From a usability perspective, the form on the slider had some issues: if you input invalid data (forgot the '@' in your email) then an error message would appear and push the fields below it off canvas and a user would have to refresh the page in order to submit the form. I offered to change the display of the error messages, but my Marketing Manager wanted a complete overhaul of the slider, completely removing the forms and focusing on our value proposition.

The new look:

The slider is animated, so best to view it in action.

The font and size of the text has been updated, we now use Roboto in headers and Open Sans for our content text, and I've increased the size of our content text for readability. I've made the text a dark grey and reserved the 'aruvio blue & green' for graphics and buttons. The objective was to create a clean, easy to understand website, and that's just what I've done.

Highlighting Product Benefits

When I can on-board, the website was mostly walls of text, and I was asked to create separate areas on each product page to draw attention to the benefits of each. So, using a plugin for bootstrap's css I made little widget-like areas that had our 'aruvio check-mark' and the list of benefits.

Call to Action: SC Magazine

A point of pride for Aruvio's GRC product is its 4 out of 5 stars from SC Magazine, so I was asked to create a call to action at the bottom of every product page to download the review from SC Magazine. This is done in pure HTML/CSS, so the text could be selectable and I could use the bootstrap button. Used my working knowledge of php to add it to every page by adding it directly to the template we use for product pages.

In-Text Call to Actions

We have a number of downloadables, and to increase user's awareness of them, I added graphical call-to-actions for a relevant downloadable on each product page.

An example of the Incident Management Call to Action

Header Banners

I created a series of banners for the heading of each product page. The icons are used to represent the different products on a number of other applications as well, including on a slide of the home page carousel, and the powerpoint deck for our sales team.
