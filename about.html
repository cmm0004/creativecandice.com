Candice McCollough is a friend, daughter, entrepreneur, excellent cook, and creative problem solver.

Where I came from

orn and raised in the South - though not any particular part of it - I first got into art and design at Auburn University after a quick major change from Industrial Design to Graphic Design. I developed a love for typography, learning as much as I could about letterforms and looking to established designers for inspiration.

Places Lived, by Year:

graduated form Auburn in May of 2012 and moved to Atlanta to work for a medium sized technology firm that specialized in document management. All of my work there was proprietary, but I began my interest in web technologies while I was there. I had never before appreciated how vast and complex the internet was - having been isolated in print-design-land at Auburn. I started learning HTML, CSS, and Javascript, and started trying to build little websites from scratch.

bout 8 months after I began working in Atlanta, I was laid off my position as a Creative Specialist due to department downsizing. This began the hardest and most challenging point of my career to date. I had been working on and off on my start-up Teas on the Loose and now poured myself into learning everything I could to build the website for it. I learned the skills needed to administer and develop a fully functional Wordpress site and used those new skills to secure my current position as a Web Designer at another technology firm in Atlanta.

My most valuable skill is my enthusiasm. Its an enthusiasm to learn new things, to dig deeper into problems, and to hunker down and get things done.

Where I Am

resently I am working in Atlanta as a Wearer of Many Hats for a small technology company specializing in Cloud Based Governance, Risk, and Compliance software. I administer, maintenance, and update our company websites, analyze and update our SEO efforts, connect our web-forms to third party API's, design marketing collateral such as datasheets and images for our social media posts, design presentations, design banners and trade-show booth materials, provide occasional desktop support to my co-workers and generally make my boss' jobs easier. I'm currently working on learning Adobe After Effects to create more materials for our marketing push.

Where I'm Going

hat is in my future? I'm not exactly sure, but I know what I want it to be. I want my start-up to take off, I want to be able to make my own hours, and dedicate myself to typography and letterpress. I want to make beautiful things. Of course, that's my pie-in-the-sky dream, my back up plan is to get into advertising, making beautiful things.

What I want most is to be in a position - both professionally and personally - where I can always be improving.

About This Site

his is my professional portfolio and personal blog site. I'm using Bootstrap's css and javascript with a few minor tweaks and a couple of Bootstrap's starter templates for the pages. Its a constant work in progress as I find new design trends I like and want to experiment with. I recently discovered Google's documentation on their javascript-based interactive graphs, so of course I had to include a few. The fonts are Helvetica and Athelas, and the beautiful drop caps are by the wildly talented Jessica Hische, my design-crush.
