Teas on the Loose is my fingers grasping at the American Dream.

soon-to-be start-up serving fine loose leaf tea on an e-commerce subscription model. This is a collaborative venture with a friend of mine; he was given administrative and legal duties, while I took on the task of creating a brand, packaging, and advertising.

The Problem

Basically, it needs everything.

he company needs a brand and identity, it needs packaging, a market differentiator, a website and a social media presence. It needs content and a blog. It needs all the legal and financial problems taken care of. The whole operation has to fit within Georgia's food and business laws. The logistics of moving product and processing payments has to be figured out. In short, it needs everything.

hotography is a big part of the company, I and my collaborator take all or own photography and use it on the blog, and the company's social media profiles. I think its extremely important to show the product and I look to cooking blogs for composition inspiration.

easonable packaging has been my biggest challenge on this project. The style needs to be 'artisan' and the packaging must be produceable in large quantities while staying in budget and stay within the Georgia Dept. of Agriculture's requirements. The solution was stamps and labels. Stamp everything that never changes on the bag, and then use a label for the information that changes, such as the name of each tea. My back-of-the-envelope calculations on all materials to create the first 600 bags is a little under $600, which is incredibly cheap for small runs of packaging materials. Just got the stamps in and am currently pulling everything I need together to create the packaging. Now I just need to build a press.
