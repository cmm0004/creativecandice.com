Teas on the Loose

Discover New and Interesting Teas

What's this all about?

Teas on the Loose is my fingers grasping at the American Dream. It's a soon-to-be start-up serving fine loose leaf tea on an e-commerce subscription model. This is a collaborative venture with a friend of mine; he was given administrative and legal duties, while I took on the task of creating a brand, packaging, and advertising.

Logo

I was asked to make something elegant. With symmetry. And floral influence:

Final logo with ITC Lubalin Graph Std for its open counters and slab serif and Fairview for a condensed 'on the'.

Color Influence

The red and grey colors were inspired by Chinese inkwash paintings which are shades of grey and the artist's monogram in red.

Business Cards

I had made some watercolor and printmaking textures for an early logo concept and decided to re-use them here for a hand-made look.

Website

The web presence for Teas on the Loose is a Wordpress installation using the Vantage theme. I made a few minor css tweaks and used the whole project as a crash-course in learning the Wordpress Content Management Framework.

See the Live Site

Packaging

Packaging has been my biggest challenge on this project. The style needs to be 'artisan' and the packaging must be produceable in large quantities while staying in budget and stay within the Georgia Dept. of Agriculture's requirements. The solution was stamps and labels. Stamp everything that never changes on the bag, and them use a label for the information that changes, such as the name of each tea.

Marketing

We decided to go with 'guerrilla' marketing. Simple, cheap, and memorable. These cards are meant to be left (along with generous tips) at restaurants for servers. A quick 'Thanks for the great service!' hand-written on the back adds a touch of personalization. This is something I am actively adding and changing.
